With us, Tydides fear'd, and urged his haste: And Menelads came, but came the last, He join'd our vessels in the Lesbian bay, While yet we doubted of our watery way; If to the right to urge the pilot's toil (The safer road), beside the Psyrian isle; Or the straight course to rocky Chios plough, And anchor under Mimas' shaggy brow? We sought direction of the power divine: The god propitious gave the guiding sign; Through the mid seas he bid our navy steer, And in Euboea shun the woes we fear. The whistling winds already waked the sky; Before the whistling winds the vessels fly, With rapid swiftness cut the liquid way, And reach Gerestus at the point of day. There hecacombs of bulls, to Neptune slain, High-flaming please the monarch of the main. The fourth day shone, when all their labours o'er, Tydides' vessels touched the wish'd-for shore.
