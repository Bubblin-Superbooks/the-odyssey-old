But when to Sunium's sacred point we came, Crown'd with the temple of the Athenian dame; Atride's pilot, Phrontes, there expired (Phrontes, of all the songs of men admired To steer the bounding bark with steady toil, When the storm thickens, and the billows boil); While yet he exercised the steerman's art, Apollo touch'd him with his gentle dart; Even with the rudder in his hand, he fell. To pay whole honours to the shades of hell, We check'd our haste, by pious office bound, And laid our old companion in the ground.
