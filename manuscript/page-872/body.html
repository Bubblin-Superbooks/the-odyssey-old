But, since till then this trial you delay, Trust it one moment to my hands to-day: Fain would I prove, before your judging eyes, What once I was, whom wretched you despise: If yet this arm its ancient force retain; Or if my woes (a long-continued train) And wants and insults, make me less than man."

Rage flash'd in lightning from the suitors' eyes, Yet mixed with terror at the bold emprise.
