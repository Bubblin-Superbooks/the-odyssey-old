With me from Scyros to the field of fame Radiant in arms the blooming hero came. When Greece assembled all her hundred states, To ripen counsels, and decide debates, Heavens! how he charm'd us with a flow of sense, And won the heart with manly eloquence! He first was seen of all the peers to rise, The third in wisdom, where they all were wise! But when, to try the fortune of the day, Host moved toward host in terrible array, Before the van, impatient for the fight, With martial port he strode, and stern delight: Heaps strew'd on heaps beneath his falchion groan'd, And monuments of dead deform'd the ground.
