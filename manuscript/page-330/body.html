The queen inquiring by what means he had the garments he then wore, be relates to her and Alcinous his departure from Calypso, and his arrival in their dominions.

The same day continues, and the book ends with the night.

The patient heavenly man thus suppliant pray'd; While the slow mules draws on the imperial maid; Through the proud street she moves, the public gaze; The turning wheel before the palace stays. With ready love her brothers, gathering round, Received the vestures, and the mules unbound.
