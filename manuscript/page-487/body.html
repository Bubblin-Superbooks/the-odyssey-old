New wine, with honey-temper'd milk, we bring, Then living waters from the crystal spring: O'er these was strew'd the consecrated flour, And on the surface shone the holy store.

"Now the wan shades we hail, the infernal gods, To speed our course, and waft us o'er the floods: So shall a barren heifer from the stall Beneath the knife upon your altars fall; So in our palace, at our safe return, Rich with unnumber'd gifts the pile shall burn; So shall a ram, the largest of the breed, Black as these regions, to Tiresias bleed.

"Thus solemn rites and holy vows we paid To all the phantom-nations of the dead; Then died the sheep: a purple torrent flow'd, And all the caverns smoked with streaming blood.
