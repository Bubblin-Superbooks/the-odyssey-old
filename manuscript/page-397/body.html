But say through what waste regions hast thou stray'd What customs noted, and what coasts survey'd; Possess'd by wild barbarians fierce in arms, Or men whose bosom tender pity warms? Say why the fate of Troy awaked thy cares, Why heaved thy bosom, and why flowed thy tears? Just are the ways of Heaven: from Heaven proceed The woes of man; Heaven doom'd the Greeks to bleed, A theme of future song! Say, then, if slain Some dear-loved brother press'd the Phrygian plain? Or bled some friend, who bore a brother's part, And claim'd by merit, not by blood, the heart?"
